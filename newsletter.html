Edition 102

Offense at Machine Speed

CrowdStrike's 2026 Global Threat Report puts the average eCrime breakout time, the window between initial access and lateral movement across the network, at 29 minutes. Mandiant's M-Trends 2026 shows adversary hand-off times have collapsed to 22 seconds. These are not abstract benchmarks. They define the operational window defenders have to detect, triage, and respond before an intrusion becomes a breach. For most organizations, that window is shorter than the time it takes to open a ticket.

This edition covers an actively exploited vulnerability in Adobe Acrobat, an unmonitored AI threat surface living inside enterprise browsers, and a wealth management firm that lost 641,000 client records with no breach notification issued. The innovation items examine the AI speed asymmetry directly, what it means when offense operates at machine speed and defense still operates at human speed. The training byte addresses the one defensive behavior that consistently closes the gap regardless of tooling budget: knowing what to do in the first 60 seconds of a suspected incident.
