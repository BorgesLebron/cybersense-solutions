About

We believe awareness is the first line of defense.

Mission

To make cybersecurity intelligence accessible, actionable, and daily.

CyberSense.Solutions was built on a straightforward premise: the gap between what threat actors know and what most organizations act on is not a technology gap — it's an intelligence gap. Our mission is to close that gap by delivering accurate, timely, and analyst-written cybersecurity intelligence to every security practitioner, IT professional, and business leader who needs it.

We don't syndicate. We don't aggregate headlines and call them intelligence. Every threat record, briefing item, and training byte in our pipeline is written and validated by our team — grounded in current CVE data, CISA advisories, vendor disclosures, and practitioner experience.

Our subscribers get the same quality of analysis that used to require a full-time threat intelligence function. That's the mission — and it's non-negotiable.

Vision

A world where organizations are never blindsided by threats they should have seen coming.

The security industry is extraordinarily good at building tools that detect and respond to threats — after they happen. CyberSense.Solutions is built around a different premise: organizations that are continuously aware of the threat landscape make better decisions, faster, before an incident forces their hand.

Our vision is a platform where intelligence, training, and culture reinforce each other in a closed loop. Daily briefings build awareness. Training builds capability. Zero-Trust simulations test whether both translate to behavior when it matters. Organizations that use all three layers aren't just better informed — they are measurably more resilient.

We measure our success not in subscriber counts, but in attacks that didn't succeed because someone on our platform knew what to look for.

Why We Exist

Because "patch Tuesday" isn't a security strategy.

Most organizations with serious security incidents share a common thread: they had the technology to prevent it, but not the timely intelligence to act on it. Threat actors move in hours. Patch cycles run in weeks. Awareness briefings, when they exist, are quarterly. The math doesn't work in defenders' favor — and it doesn't have to be that way.

We exist because the security practitioner at a regional hospital, a mid-market financial firm, or a state agency deserves the same quality of threat intelligence as the Fortune 100 company with a 20-person threat intel team. Intelligence shouldn't be a budget line item only large organizations can afford.

We also exist because security culture isn't built in annual training sessions. It's built in daily habits. The training byte you read this morning, the briefing you forwarded to your team last week, the phishing simulation that caught your CFO — these are the building blocks of a genuinely cyber-resilient organization.

The Story

Built by practitioners who got tired of watching preventable incidents.

CyberSense.Solutions grew out of direct frustration with the state of security awareness in the organizations our founders worked with. After years in incident response, penetration testing, and security operations, the pattern was consistent: organizations weren't breached because their defenses were inadequate. They were breached because someone on their team didn't know what a specific threat looked like — or didn't know it was actively being exploited that week.

The platform launched with a single product: a daily email briefing written by security analysts for security professionals. The response was immediate. Subscribers forwarded it to their teams. IT directors shared it with their boards. The format worked because it respected the reader's time and treated them as a practitioner, not a compliance checkbox.

From that foundation, we built the Threat Radar, the Intel Brief, the training platform, and ultimately the Zero-Trust Culture Platform for enterprise teams. Every product we've added reflects the same principle: intelligence is only valuable if it changes behavior. We're in the business of changing behavior.
